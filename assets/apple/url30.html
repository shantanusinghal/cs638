iTunes Review

Making the shift from teen sensation to mature artist can be tough, but on her second album, Fearless, an 18-year-old Taylor Swift did it with style. The songs ring out with angst and delight in equal measure. Guitars chime and beats bounce as Swift sails through the title track, “Hey Stephen,” and “You Belong With Me” with boundless joy. These moments glow all the more alongside thoughtful tunes like “Fifteen” (a knowing glance back at high school) and “White Horse” (a snapshot of lost innocence).

Customer Reviews

Great job Taylor!
     
by Freesi@

Taylor's new album is great! Here's my track by track review! 1) Fearless 9/10 Awesome song! The chorus is amazing and it stays in your head. My favourite line: And I don't know why but with you I'd dance, in a storm in my best dress fearless. 2) Fifteen 7/10 All about Taylor's first day. She goes to school and meets her new best friend, checks out the senior guy and basically writes about everything you feel and think when you're 15. Very cute. 3) Love Story 10/10 Her second single, very fairytale-ish, check out the music video. We wish Romeo and Juliet had such a happy ending. 4) Hey Stephen 8/10 Taylor writes about her crush on this guy called Stephen. It's very very sweet and cute. 5) White Horse 9/10 A slower song that's sorta opposite Love Story. It's about Taylor waking up and realizing that not all fairytales come true. 6) You Belong With Me 9/10 A very well written song, I love the lyrics. It's basically Taylor telling this guy that she's so much better for him than the girl he's going out with. Really fun. 7) Breathe 10/10 I can relate to this song so much and I've lost count of the number of times I've played it. Great lyrics, great tune and oh did I mention it features Colbie Caillat? 8) Tell Me Why 8/10 Taylor asking a guy why he's so mean to her. Very fast paced and fierce. You'll have to hear it to get it. 9) You're Not Sorry 10/10 LOVELY. I think she wrote a song called "You Don't Have To" and it covers the exact same topics in this song. So now I highly doubt that "You Don't Have To" would be released officially. Sad, I really liked it but this song is great too. 10) The Way I Loved You 9/10 Ahh...interesting. She describes this perfect guy and we assume that she's written this song for him. But in the chorus she talks to another one and claims that she loves him more. 11) Forever and Always 8/10 Another good song. (Duh, it's Taylor) It is Taylor reminding a guy about his promise to her, (FOREVER and ALWAYS). It's good but blends in with her other songs. 12) The Best Day 7/10 A song from Taylor to her mom. It's very sweet and not many girls have this sort of a relationship with their parents anymore. 13) Change 8/10 Her first single and a great way to end an album! Overall, Taylor did a fantastic job! I definitely think this is just as good as her first one, if not better. I can't wait to see what she releases next!
