Eminem's Relapse... worth the wait? Track for track review:
     
by redsoxruletheeast

After Em took a long break after his disappointing last outting "Encore", he finally returns with "Relapse". After all these years with all the hype building up, was it worth the wait? Here's a track for track review of the album: 1. Dr. West: Opening skit. Nice intro to the album, sets the overall tone quite nicely. 2. 3 A.M.: The third single for the album. Solid beat, great lyrics, stupid accent. Ever since "Encore" with tracks such as "A*s Like That" he's used this voice on various occassions. Not a bad track but it could of been better if he lost the accent. 3/5 3. My Mom: After all this time I actually had no problem listening to a track about Em's mom, yet again. Nice Dre beat which sounds quite like "What's the Difference". Some humorous lines and a nice flow. One of the more entertaining tracks. 3.5/5 4. Insane: The most bizarre track on here. Talks about his stepfather molesting him in great detail yet you can't help but bob your head to it. He kills it on the second verse and if you're looking for that intensity Em's known for, look no further. 4/5 5. Bagpipes from Baghdad: Track dissing Mariah Carey and Nick Cannon. One of my least favorite on the album. Nice beat though. 2.5/5 6. Hello: Not a bad beat, didn't get into this one too much though. One of the more forgettable tracks. 2.5/5 7. Tonya: Sets the tone for the next track. 8. Same Song & Dance: This track definitely grew on me. Talks about the people who have fallen victim to him. Solid beat, decent flow and chorus as well. 4/5 9. We Made You: Kind of kills the overall tone of the album. One of his humorous tracks. Not much else to say. 3/5 10. Medicine Ball: Sick beat, catchy chorus, nice lyrics, Christopher Reeve dissing. Album is really consistent this point forward. 4/5 11. Paul: One of the phone calls as heard on his previous albums. 12. Stay Wide Awake: Great hook, nice beat, good punchlines. Definitely Slim Shady status. 4/5 13. Old Time's Sake: One of the singles of the album. Second best one next to "Beautiful". Nothing crazy, solid collab with Dre. 3.5/5 14. Must Be the Ganja: Solid beat, mediocre track. Nothing too special. Not the worst but not the best either. 3/5 15. Mr. Mathers: Opening to "Deja Vu" 16. Deja Vu: One of the best tracks on the album. Definitely brought the fire on this one. Sick beat, really digs in deep about his addiction and just kills it. 5/5 17. Beautiful: Arguably the best track on this album. Em actually talks about some real sh*t and that's when he shines the best. No dumb accent, no stupid hook, no guest appearances. Just 100% real and from the heart. 5/5 18. Crack a Bottle: Could of went without this track. Kills the mood after hearining "Beautiful". Em would of had a serious album on his hands if he dropped this track and "We Made You". Alright for a club maybe, that's about it though. 2.5/5 19. Steve Berman: Em pays another visit to his manager. Shows that another album is on the way. 20. Underground / Ken Kaniff: One of the best tracks on here. Supposed to be his powerhouse encore and it comes off as just that, but it seems like it could of been a tad more polished. Flow sometimes doesn't match the intensity of the beat and it seems like it doesn't mesh that well at times, but it's still a bada*s track nonetheless. Highly recommend this one and it shows you that Em is back and is here to stay without just focusing on getting played on the radio or on MTV. He's sober and he's as angry as ever. 5/5 OVERALL: 8/10

OrionBC
     
by OrionBC

OMG -- The Master is BACK! Except -- wait, no. He's ISN'T. Is it just me, or does this albun make you LONG for the days of "Criminal," "Just Don't Give a F--k," "Rock Bottom, " and "The Way I Am"?!? Eminem used to stand for something. He used to MEAN something. Hell, he used to SAY something! This album, for the most part, is just ridiculous, radio-friendly, celeb-bashing garbage. And you know it. You just don't want to admit it to yourself. Hey, neither did I. I am -- or WAS, for the longest time -- a HUUUUUUGE Em fan. But THIS? Well, it's unacceptable. And I think Em knows it, too. Of course, that said, he'll still make his millions, and all the pre-adolescent nut-suckers will be on his jock, calling this "the best album he's ever done!" and what have you. But we -- the TRUE FANS -- know that it, sadly, is not. Far from it. Sorry to be that guy; but someone had to say what we were all thinking.

Em is back...but did we really miss him?
     
by Richard H

Yeah so everyone's buzzing about Eminem returning to the rap scene, but for me, I believe his previous albums TMM-LP and The Eminem Show are far superior to this album. That terrible 'accent' that haunts most of the tracks is annoying and totally takes away from the magic. Yeah, there are some gems here, "INSANE" and some hits, but mostly misses. I know people will buy anything Em puts out, and they say its been forever, but what's more important, releasing something quickly, or releasing something magical. His previous records were magic, this, not so much.
