Customer Reviews

Great and refreshing!
     
by Mister Vain

Cascada often gets lots of hate from people claiming that they only make cover songs (blame their record labels) and that their music all use the same beats. This song is the result of those complaints: a summertime dance-pop smash! It debuted at #1 in the United Kingdom, and though it's going to start off slow in the USA because of a lack of exposure (like any of their other songs), it's destined to pick up quickly just like Everytime We Touch. A mix of Britney Spears and Lady GaGa, this song was released to follow a current trend of dance-pop, and it does its job very well! My only complaint is against the record label ROBBINS ENTERTAINMENT. First of all, they priced this release at $9.99. A maxi-single release on iTunes should be at MOST $5.99. $9.99 is a total rip-off! Secondly, every song in this release costs $1.29 which, without a doubt, will limit their iTunes sales. If Robbins really really wants to charge $1.29, it should be limited to the Radio Edit, with the rest at 99 cents. Furthermore, they chose to limit consumers' choices by putting THREE versions of the Wideboys remix while not including the Rob Mayth and Ultrabeat remixes (which are Hands Up style) which would appeal to Cascada's original fans! Unfortunately, the people at Robbins are IDIOTS and won't learn from their mistakes until it's too late.

Biography

Formed: 2004 in Bonn, Germany

Genre: Dance

Years Active: '00s, '10s

Originally called Cascade until legal conflicts forced them to change their name, Cascada is the slick, uplifting Euro-dance trio featuring producers DJ Manian and Yanou along with vocalist Natalie Horler, although Horler is sometimes presented as Cascada herself. Horler was born in Bonn, Germany, to English parents and grew up loving all sorts of music.
