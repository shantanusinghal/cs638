Editors’ Notes

Filled with torch songs influenced by hip-hop, Lana Del Rey wrote and recorded Born to Die in a chilling fashion. These noir-shaded numbers are mostly built on orchestral accouterments and subtle vocal samples. Del Rey’s husky narrative lingers like smoke clouds, leaving wafts of rhythmic phrasings delivered by an icy, heartbroken femme fatale.

Customer Reviews

Classier than anything I've ever heard
     
by Deltrese

Let me preface this by saying that I would have never been caught dead with this kind of music before I heard Lizzy Grant aka Lana Del Rey. But I just had to hear Video Games once and I have been - this is such a strong word to toss around but it's totally necessary here - ADDICTED. Her voice is an absolute shell-shock and far too real to shake away the goosebumps I get every time I listen to one of her songs. She has risen past the sex appeal iconicism that is packaged with every new female artist (though I must say she is drop-dead gorgeous) and actually offered up competent, soulful, haunting music to the public. Definitely check out "Born to Die", "Video Games", "Blue Jeans", "Off to the Races", and "Summertime Sadness". Forget it, buy the whole thing. It's no doubt Lana Del Rey has slayed me and she'll do the same to you.

Biography

Born: June 21, 1986 in Lake Placid, NY

Genre: Alternative

Years Active: '10s

Lana Del Rey envisioned a Southern Californian dream world constructed out of sad girls and bad boys, manufactured melancholy and genuine glamour, and then she came to embody this fantasy. At first, her stylized noir-pop garnered skeptical sneers — the rise of her 2012 debut Born to Die was impeded by a tentative live debut on Saturday Night Live — but Lana Del Rey proved to be tougher than her soft exterior suggested.
