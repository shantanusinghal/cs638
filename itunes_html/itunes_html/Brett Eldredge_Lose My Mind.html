Editors’ Notes

Wacka-chicka guitars, shuffly disco-pop beats, and a soulful falsetto don’t usually make for much of a country song, but that’s just fine by Brett Eldredge, who gives the genre a bit of a makeover on Ilinois. Witness that funky strut on “You Can’t Stop Me” or the galloping rock of “Fire”—these tunes waste no time getting down to business. Still, when Eldredge wants to revel in classic country vibes, he does so confidently, getting reflective on “Lose It All” or taking it home on the big-band stomper “Illinois.”

Biography

Born: March 23, 1986 in Paris, IL

Genre: Country

Years Active: '10s

Singing country music with a soulful edge and more than a dash of pop polish, Brett Eldredge was born in Paris, Illinois on March 23, 1986, and grew up listening to singers Ray Charles, Frank Sinatra, and Bobby Darin. After hearing Brooks & Dunn at the age of 16, he became fascinated by country music, a fascination that stayed with him when he began attending Elmhurst College in Chicago.
