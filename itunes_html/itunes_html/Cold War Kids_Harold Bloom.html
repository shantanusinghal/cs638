Editors’ Notes

Since their 2006 debut, Robbers & Cowards, Cold War Kids have become increasingly focused studio perfectionists; their sound tightens with each release. With erstwhile Modest Mouse members Dann Gallucci (guitar) and Joe Plummer (drums) in the mix, the band's sound is bold, muscular, and polished on its fifth album. With its outsized chorus and "We Will Rock You" beat, "First" is ready for arenas, while "All This Could Be Yours" is a barrelhouse rocker with a sing-along hook. The second half is more subdued, from the U2-worthy "Nights & Weekends" to the forlorn ballad "Harold Bloom."

Customer Reviews

5 Albums In
     
by Smquintan

I like what the first two singles promise on the new album. I’ve never been the type who’s dismissed every album after Robbers & Cowards. After all, my favorite album is Loyalty to Loyalty. Each CWK album has (to me at least) has had some different to offer and something different to say. While Mine is Yours had too much production polish on it, it was by no means an awful album. Dear Miss Lonely Hearts evoked original CWK while mixing everything they’ve done before. Now Hold My Home is tackling a new area. Radio pop, but with that CWK spin to it. Dismissing everything this band has done because it will never be like R&C is a silly thing to do. I for one can say that CWK is still one of my favorite bands.

Previews sound promising... so far.
     
by scratchyournose

The two songs released as of 8/24/14 are catchier and more radio-ready than anything CWK have done in a while. I doubt they will ever top (or even recreate) the perfect balances of emotion and atmosphere from the Robbers & Cowards and Loyalty To Loyalty eras, but honestly I would rather hear catchy radio-friendly stuff than have to listen to the meandering, self-indulgent crap on the last two albums.

I like evolution
     
by Cana241

Although yes, they have changed their style from their 2 first cd's, I am still loving what I'm hearing. There are bands that change and make things worse (The Black Keys), but this guys now what they are doing. I love how they have evolved, I'm getting this cd no matter what.

Biography

Formed: Fullerton, CA

Genre: Alternative

Years Active: '00s, '10s

Long Beach, California's Cold War Kids make music with roots that go deep and wide, embracing influences as diverse as Bob Dylan, Billie Holiday, Jeff Buckley, and the Velvet Underground. Vocalist/guitarist/pianist Nathan Willett, bassist Matt Maust, drummer Matt Aveiro, and guitarist Jonnie Russell began crafting their soulful, blues-inflected take on indie rock as Cold War Kids in 2004, recording demos in Los Angeles soon after they formed.
