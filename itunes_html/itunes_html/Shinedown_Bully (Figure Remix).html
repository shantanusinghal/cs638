Odd but think about it before you bash it
     
by Enemies_20to1

At first glance this appears to be a very un-Shinedown like move. However, as this band grows and gets bigger its inevitable that the tentacles of corporate music will sneak in and pull stunts like this to maximize profits. This was probably some suits idea...wouldn't be shocked if the band had little to do with it. Or,maybe on an off day they were messing around on the mac and liked what came out and released it...nobody is forcing you to buy it. The 2nd and 3rd ones arent all that awful although Bully isn't a fav of mine anyway. I'd love a raw stripped down enemies if they wanna go the remix route. As a die hard fan any and all new SD product is GOOD. Some of it won't be stellar but at least they never stop trying/creating/pushing the edge. This is only if you are a Shinedown completist like me (record execs do this junk because of us) or if Bully is your all time favorite song. The fact they already have the follow-up to Amaryllis recorded to me gives them the leeway to just toss something out there for poops and giggles. Still the best band on earth!

Biography

Formed: Jacksonville, FL

Genre: Rock

Years Active: '00s, '10s

Melodic hard rockers Shinedown hail from Jacksonville, Florida and originally featured vocalist Brent Smith, guitarist Jasin Todd, bassist Brad Stewart, and drummer Barry Kerch. Snapped up by Atlantic Records during the early-2000s flurry of post-Creed and Nickelback signings, the group released its debut album, Leave a Whisper, in 2003.
