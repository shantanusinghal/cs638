Editors’ Notes

Anchored by "Feel So Close" and "We Found Love"—inarguably two of the decade's biggest EDM tracks—Calvin Harris' 18 Months cemented the Scottish producer as one of the biggest superstars of his generation. That album's bronze medal goes to "Sweet Nothing"; the track's enormous beats and synth arpeggios are matched only by Florence Welch's monster pipes. Diplo and Grandtheft deliver a trap remix, flanking Welch's vocal with lurching beats, while Dutch megastar Tiësto applies his signature trance theatrics. For an out-there take, try Burns' dubby, abstract remix. The pounding dubsteb version, complete with concussive drops, is delivered by the Brazil twosome Dirtyloud.

The Remixes

All are pretty great the Diplo + Grandtheft is a good trap mix, the Burns is more relaxing, Dirtyloud is a typical dubstep and Drum & Bass mix nothing too special and the Tiesto and Qulinez are fun dance house tracks but Tiesto is the clear winner.

Diplo and Burns
     
by whotheowl

Breath new life into this song.. Diplo is starting to get repetitive with the trap, but at least at this point its still more original then the "Tiesto treatment" which is EXACTLY what you'd expect. And the Burns remix is successful in actually relaxing the song a bit and changing the vibe entirely. Don't bother with the other 2 unless you're all about crazy electro and dub step
