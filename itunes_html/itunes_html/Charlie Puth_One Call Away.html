by Scottish Locke

I was first introduced to Charlie Puth like most people were, from See You Again. The song was emotional and heartfelt on his end, even if you don't like the writing for the music itself you can't deny the Charlie has an incredible singing voice. Well naturally I was fascinated with this mysterious character who is linked to one of the biggest songs of the year. I looked him up on iTunes and was surprised to see that he was relatively unknown. Only credits he had his name or a featuring credit, and a recently released EP. Well I listen to samples of the songs on the EP, and I was excited so I purchased the EP. Needless to say the songs are phenomenal with great writing beautiful music and wonderful singing. The songs on the EP are the most listened to songs on my entire library. I can say regardless of all the negative stigmatism against him simply because he's a new artist, I think the Charlie Puth has a lot to offer the most current artists just don't have. I think Charlie's immensely talented with beautifully inspired roots, and you should definitely give him a listen.

Biography

Born: December 2, 1991 in Rumson, NJ

Genre: Pop

Years Active: '10s

Pop singer and songwriter Charlie Puth began his career with acoustic covers uploaded to YouTube. An early supporter was Ellen DeGeneres, who signed the Berklee College alum — along with his duet partner, Emily Luther — to her Warner Bros.-affiliated ElevenEleven label. Though nothing materialized from that deal, Puth eventually signed with Atlantic. In early 2015, he released his debut single "Marvin Gaye" — a duet with Meghan Trainor.
