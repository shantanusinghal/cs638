Editors’ Notes

Even though he is a self-professed student of classicists like Merle Haggard and George Strait, Eric Church puts the old country formulas through a lot of unexpected twists on his sophomore album, Carolina. Whereas most young singers extol the virtues of small town life, “Where She Told Me To Go” uses a small roadside hamlet as a metaphor for the mistakes the narrator has made.

Sensational Second Record
     
by Junior_EBR

Oftentimes, artists are made or broken by their sophomore release. With Carolina, Eric Church builds upon his status as storyteller and witty lyricist. While this albums most popular song is "Smoke a Little Smoke," the emotional appeal of "Love Your Love the Most" and personal openness of "Those I've Loved" rival and in many ways surpass it in terms of musicality and message. Overall, Carolina is the album rich in both depth and radio-friendliness Church needed to cement his place in Country Music.

Biography

Born: May 3, 1977 in Granite Falls, NC

Genre: Country

Years Active: '00s, '10s

Country singer/songwriter/guitarist Eric Church grew up in Granite Falls, North Carolina, and began singing as a child. At 13, he started writing songs, later teaching himself to play guitar. While attending college at Appalachian State, he formed a band, the Mountain Boys, that played around western North Carolina.
