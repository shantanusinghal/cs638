Editors’ Notes

Urban is sweet and gentle, but these pieces exhibit an underlying tension that keeps them from becoming maudlin. The outstanding examples are “Somebody Like You” and “Who Wouldn’t Wanna Be Me,” the exceptional one-two combination that opens the album. Urban clearly loves the crack of a snare and the pulse of a kick drum, and “What About Me,” “Jeans On” and “You Won” are all about the balance between a pretty melody and a sharp rhythm.

Biography

Born: October 26, 1967 in Whangarei, New Zealand

Genre: Country

Years Active: '90s, '00s, '10s

Although born in New Zealand and raised in nearby Australia, Keith Urban made his biggest splash in Nashville, where he helped rewrite the rules of contemporary country music. By embracing drum loops and elements of Top 40 pop, Urban wrote songs that appealed to a wide audience, effectively satisfying his Nashville fans without alienating those more accustomed to pop music.
