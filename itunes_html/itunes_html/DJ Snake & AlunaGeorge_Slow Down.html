Album Review

Guilty pleasures gush out of Amsterdam Trap Music, Vol. 2, an EP where Amsterdam trio Yellow Claw continue to take trap music to the stadium, with all the garishness and bass drops that implies. Here, Wiz Khalifa acts surprised that his "trap is going techno" on the silly and killer opener "Techno," while Jamaican superstar Beenie Man declares he's a triumphant "Dancehall Soldier" over a bucking bronco of a trap beat. Two more bangers close the set and spew out more party talk than Scooter and Dada Life combined, but if you're going to go trap, go big, or extra, extra large if you're Yellow Claw. Check their Amsterdam Twerk Music EP series for more of this over-the-top club mayhem.

Biography

Born: 2010 in Amsterdam, Netherlands

Genre: Dance

Years Active: '10s

Taking the over the top attitude of stadium techno acts like Scooter, mixing it with the profanity-laced party style of Dada Life, then filtering it all through the cutting-edge dance styles of trap and neo-electro, Amsterdam trio Yellow Claw don't consider themselves a band or project but a "partyconcept."
