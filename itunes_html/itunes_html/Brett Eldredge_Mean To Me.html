FINALLY!! :D
     
by Trin96

If you are debating about buying this album, BUY IT NOW! Don't pick and choose songs, but buy them all as each is remarkable. I have been waiting 3 years for this album. Finally I can listen to it constantly, and trust me I am and will be doing so. I have loved Brett for a long time, and to have this brilliant masterpiece available to my ears is quite fantastic. This album shows that Brett is truly versatile. He can sing something as fun as Don't Ya, On and On, or Tell Me Where To Park and turn right around and sing touching heart felt songs such as Raymond, Bring You Back, One Mississippi, and Go On Without Me. I fell in love with his deep emotional voice with Raymond and since then he has not disappointed. I have a feeling he never will disappoint me either. Brett is true talent. His songs are honest, emotional, and they drag you in. He cannot only sing, but he also writes engrossing songs. Very few artists can say that they wrote/cowrote the majority of the songs on the album. I hope this album brings him to greater heights. Raymond was the true beginning for this man, butDon't Ya is the beginning of a growing career. A career where I hope to see Brett Eldredge do remarkably well. Not only does Brett have talent, but now he has an album that showcases it.

Biography

Born: March 23, 1986 in Paris, IL

Genre: Country

Years Active: '10s

Singing country music with a soulful edge and more than a dash of pop polish, Brett Eldredge was born in Paris, Illinois on March 23, 1986, and grew up listening to singers Ray Charles, Frank Sinatra, and Bobby Darin. After hearing Brooks & Dunn at the age of 16, he became fascinated by country music, a fascination that stayed with him when he began attending Elmhurst College in Chicago.
