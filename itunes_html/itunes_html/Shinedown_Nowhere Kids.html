I highly encourage Shinedown fans to buy this album. The vocals are as clear as ever, great sound and as always, very catchy. This album clearly sets the bar for any future albums released by this group. Unity is a great for multiple plays, and I'm Alright has a great orchestrated sound. Never fails...
     
by DWalkin08

Shinedown has managed to completely cover every base of the rock genre with this album. You got your hard rocking, energy filled anthems such as 'Bully', 'Enemies', and 'Nowhere Kids'; along with a good variety of some more radio friendly tunes. It's a perfect mix for anyone who is a fan of any type of rock. I'm so excited to see them going out on a limb and experimenting with different sounds that haven't been on a Shinedown album before, such as the outstanding 'Through the Ghost'. Being a fan of the band from the Leave a Whisper days, I can honestly say I couldn't be more excited with how this album turned out. I'm sure the haters will completely flock to this album to tear apart their hard work and call them radio sellouts, but at the end of the day they are bold and willing to try new things to add to their already amazing collection of albums. I am beyond looking forward to seeing them twice this Spring on their first round of tours in support of the album. To sum it all up in their own words, Shinedown didn't take this album "Back against the wall". Excellent work guys!

Biography

Formed: Jacksonville, FL

Genre: Rock

Years Active: '00s, '10s

Melodic hard rockers Shinedown hail from Jacksonville, Florida and originally featured vocalist Brent Smith, guitarist Jasin Todd, bassist Brad Stewart, and drummer Barry Kerch. Snapped up by Atlantic Records during the early-2000s flurry of post-Creed and Nickelback signings, the group released its debut album, Leave a Whisper, in 2003.
