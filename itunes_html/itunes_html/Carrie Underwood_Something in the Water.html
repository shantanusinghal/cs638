Underwood confirms there’s definitely “Something in the Water”
     
by DSM112

As Country music’s reigning queen moves into the next phase of her life, she takes the opportunity to thank her fans with the faith-based power ballad “Something in the Water.” Soon to be new mom Carrie Underwood releases the lead single from her forthcoming ‘Greatest Hits: Decade #1’ (December 9) with a joyful bang.

“Water” harkens back to some of the brightest spots of her career, as it shares similarities in content and structure with the massive hits “Jesus, Take the Wheel” and “So Small.” As usual, the lyrics in “Water” are meaningful, as they tell a genuine story about how a life was changed by a baptism (“And now I’m singing along to Amazing Grace / Can’t nobody wipe this smile off my face / Got joy in my heart, angels on my side / Thank God about it, I saw and let it go”). The song boasts writing credits from Chris DeStefano, Brett James and Underwood herself. And of course, there is no better singer than Underwood to deliver these lyrics, with her seasoned vocals sounding as great as ever.

As “Water” progresses, a very fitting gospel choir joins in with “Amazing Grace,” serving as a supporting harmony with Underwood’s own chorus. The incorporation of “Grace” is perfect, as it acts as both a part of the storyline and a background sample.

“Something in the Water” is absolutely the ideal single to kick off the second decade of hits for Carrie Underwood, and the inspirational track will surely become an instant classic among her already expansive songbook.
