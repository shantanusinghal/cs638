Editors’ Notes

The second installment in signings by the French electronic label Kitsuné comes from its cofounder Gildas Loaëc. Along with his partners Masaya Kuroki and Åbäke (a London-based design company), Loaëc founded Kitsuné in 2002 with the idea of combining fashion and music. It’s fitting, then, that Madeon’s “Shuriken” starts the party with stylishly Parisian electro-disco that grooves like the lovechild of Air and Daft Punk. The A-Trak remix of The Rapture’s “How Deep Is Your Love” gets the retro house treatment, replete with piano-pounding stop breaks, handclap rhythms, and wonky bass lines. The JBAG remix of “Cairo” by Kamp! similarly looks to the past for inspiration, sounding as if Erasure’s Vince Clarke and Andy Bell had produced dance music for underground warehouse parties in circa-1988 Chicago. Taking a more modern approach to dance floor magic, Bufi’s “Happy Robot” is a nightclub gem with modulated voices and a blissful bass line.
