Editors’ Notes

The soundtrack to Baz Luhrmann’s adaptation of F. Scott Fitzgerald’s novel is laden with synthesizers and samplers, not saxophones. By filling the album with new music by Beyoncé, Jack White, will.i.am., and Jay-Z (who also serves as the executive producer), it makes a shrewd parallel between Jazz Era glitz and the indulgent bling of contemporary pop charts.

Great Soundtrack but...
     
by Andrew Fella

This is what I remember movie soundtracks to look and sound like. It's stacked with great songs by great artists through and through. My only problem is that they're missing the band Filter's cover of 'Happy Together'? Honestly, it was that song that pulled me in (during the trailer) that made me go, "Wow, Now this movie looks great"! Why did they exclude the very song that seems to be the mood and the theme of the film?
