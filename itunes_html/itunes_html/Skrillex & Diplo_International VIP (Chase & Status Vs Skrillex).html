Customer Reviews

The Legends Return
     
by davidmcalhany

With a heavier and darker sound than their previous endeavors, Chase & Status come in at full force with their third studio album. Highlights in this album are noted as Machine Gun (with some help from Pusha T) and a relentless bass-wobbling remix from the one and only Sonny Moore on the International (Chase & Status VS Skrillex Remix). If your'e looking for an album with diversity get this now.

Excellent Album!
     
by Jordan Ibe

As soon as i heard the single "Count On Me" I was so ready for this album to drop! And it did not disappoint. This album brings back many classic 90s sounds, and at the same time having great soulfull drum & bass tracks or rap collabs.
My highlights would be "Count on Me" "Deeper Devotion" "Alive" and of course "Machine Gun"

Biography

Formed: 2003 in London, England

Genre: Electronic

Years Active: '00s, '10s

Chase & Status — consisting of dance DJs, multi-platinum artists, and record-label owners Saul Milton and Will Kennard — earned their stripes after remixing the Capleton track "Duppy Man" to success in 2005. From there, the duo signed to Andy C.'s Ram Records imprint and began with a pair of club hits — in 2007 with the double A-sided "Hurt You" b/w "Sell Me Your Soul" and 2008's "Take Me Away" — that also reached the top of the U.K. dance charts.
