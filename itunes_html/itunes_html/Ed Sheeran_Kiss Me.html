Editors’ Notes

British tunesmith Ed Sheeran jumps right into his lead single, “The A Team,” a catchy and melancholy narrative about a junkie prostitute on a downward spiral. In “Wake Me Up,” his lyrics take on a rhetorically conversational approach, earning the adoration of his muse with pop culture references and witty confessionals; it's sung like a handwritten letter signed and sealed over spare piano notes. There's live beatbox accompaniment in the pitter-patter backdrop of the harmonious “Small Bump” and the Justin Timberlake–inspired “You Need Me, I Don't Need You” (which boasts more a cappella rhythm tracks than a Bobby McFerrin album).
